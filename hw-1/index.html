The Wonderful World of Trigonometry

Trigonometry is a branch of mathematics that studies relationships between side lengths and angles of triangles. While it might seem daunting at first, it is the foundation for everything from architecture to video game development.

The Core Functions

The three primary functions in trigonometry are Sine, Cosine, and Tangent. These functions help us find unknown distances or angles using a right-angled triangle.

Key Trigonometric Ratios:

  • Sine (sin): Opposite / Hypotenuse
  • Cosine (cos): Adjacent / Hypotenuse
  • Tangent (tan): Opposite / Adjacent

Common Applications

We use trigonometry in the real world more often than you might think. Without it, many modern technologies simply wouldn't work.

  1. Measuring the height of a building without climbing it.
  2. Navigating planes through wind currents.
  3. Creating realistic 3D graphics in gaming.
  4. Understanding sound and light waves.
